Space Odyssey

High Concept

A rendition of the classic shmup genre. Control a small spaceship and traverse the vastness of space.
Avoid obstacles and shoot down enemy spaceships to survive as long as you can

Genres

  • Shooter
  • Action

Platform

  • Desktop Web

Story

You are a rogue space pilot from the Star Galactic Empire. You don't enjoy your current life and decide to flee the planet of Azeron. There are no more places to call home as you fly endlessly across space. Turns out, you don't really have a good reputation with the space pilots of the empire and they will seek to destroy you. Will you be able to find solitude through the vastness of space?

Goals

My goals for this project was to be able to create a nearly sellable game with the tools given to us. At first I didn't really understand JavaScript and Pixijs as much so Through doing this project, I wanted to be able to solidify my undestanding of JavaScript. Game wise, my goals were pretty simple. Make a simple and smooth game experience that exceeds the circle blast homework. I also wanted to make this game portfolio worthy and something that can show other people my skills.

Process

When first told about project 3, I already knew that I wanted to make a shmup/bullet hell/space invaders kind of game. This type of game is has simple mechanics but can still feel fun to play. Even before I did the cirlce blast hw I already had an idea. Since I don't think that I am a creative person, it was hard to come up with an original game idea. I like to find inspiration from other games and build my own version of it. That was kind of the planning process for this project.

The circle blast hw helped a lot in starting my code. I also used Dower Chin's tutorial series to get m started. I started with the simple stuff first like building the scene, then adding geometry to the scene. After that I started playing around with what I can do to the geometry. My first focus was creating the player. I started off with the simple code like movement and then slowly implemented more mechanics like shooting.

After I was satisfied with the player I moved on to the objects and enemies the player would need to avoid. I decided to use asteroids as it relates to my space theme. Like those top down shmups, I programmed asteroids to come down from the top of the screen at random locations at certain intervals. The enemy space shapes were programmed similarly but I wanted the enemies to be able to shoot and hit the player. This wasn't particularly hard as I already programmed the player to shoot so I use the same sort of program and created a function in the enemy class to allow the enemies to fire bullets.

Once the player and the enemies are able to do what they need to do, the third major step is to make them interact with eachother. First was making the sprites and bullets able to collide with eachother. Since the cirlce blast hw already had a collision function, I reused it in my code. Another function I reused form circle blast was the clamp method to keep the player within the scene.

At this point, we have a playable game. Now it's just time to polish the game and make it feel more like a game. I started by implementing the score system and the life system. Then I started to add scaling difficulty since the game was pretty easy and gets repetitive. I thought about how I would make the game harder as the game went on and ultimately I decided on scaling the difficulty based on the score. As the score gets higher I would make the asteroids/enemies spawn more often, move faster, move diagonally, and made the enemies shoot more bullets. Lastly in this process, I added a start and game over scene that both will lead back to the game scene. Now we have a game in our hands.

lastly, I implented powerups like I said I would in my proposal. The three powerups I decided on having were: Increase shoot speed, shoot 2 additional bullets forward, and shoot two additional bullets that go diagonally from the player. I implemented them similarly to the enemies. I made their spawn chance really low as these powerups do actually help a lot. I decided to make the bullet powerups as a short timed buff as they were too good. I have now implemented all the mechanics of the game.

The final process was adding in the assets and adding CSS to the cite. I found a really good asset pack that has pretty much everything I needed. I replaced everything with the assets and added sound and animations to spice up the game. Sound really does make a difference. Finally I finished up the css of the page to make it appealing and relevent to the theme. At this point everything is pretty much done and ready. Now it's time to finish up writing this document.

Requirements

The fully utilized pixijs for the game and some DOM for outside the game area. The game fully works with no errors and is simple and easy to use.

I used an asset pack for the most of the visuals. Game uses consistent design and fits the theme. Elements are legible and readable. Shouldn't be too hard to figure out whats going on on the page. Not nearly responsive but can still play on a small screen size.
